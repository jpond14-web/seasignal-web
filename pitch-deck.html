The Problem

1.9 million seafarers move 90% of global trade. They're the most connected industry's most disconnected workforce.

Blind Decisions

Seafarers sign 6-12 month contracts with zero intel on the vessel, company, or pay norms. Bad contracts mean months trapped at sea.

Exploitation

Wage theft, illegal contract terms, unpaid overtime. No centralised way to verify what's standard or report violations safely.

Isolation

Months at sea with no professional network, no mentorship, no community. Mental health crises go untracked and unreported.

There is no LinkedIn, no Glassdoor, no professional network for seafarers. The tools that exist are employer-controlled. SeaSignal is 100% crew-side.

SeaSignal is three things

1. Decision Engine

Anonymous company reviews, pay transparency data, vessel ratings, and contract checking tools.

Answer: Where should I work? What should I accept? What am I worth?

2. Trust Layer

Verified credentials, certification tracking, third-party verification API. Seafarers own and control their professional identity.

Answer: Can I prove my qualifications? Can others verify me?

3. Protection Network

Fatigue monitoring, wellness tracking, MLC rights reference, incident logging, mental health resources, and community support.

Answer: Am I safe? Am I okay? Where can I get help?

Core rule: No employer access — ever.

Architecturally enforced. Not a policy — a constraint in the database.

Growth Strategy

Network effects built into every feature. Each user makes the platform more valuable for the next.

Viral Loops

  • "Invite Your Crew" — after adding crew history, prompt to invite shipmates from that vessel
  • Port Beacon — seafarers in port see who else is there. Natural word-of-mouth at shore leave meetups
  • Verification sharing — platform token lets others verify you, driving awareness to non-users
  • Company pages are public — SEO captures "is [company] good to work for?" searches

Distribution Channels

  • Maritime unions — ITF, Nautilus International. Welfare tools align with their mission
  • Maritime colleges — cadets start tracking certs and sea time from day one
  • Welfare organisations — Sailors' Society, Mission to Seafarers. They need digital tools for their beneficiaries
  • Chaplains & port welfare visitors — install SeaSignal on crew phones during port visits
